# Threat Model

This document identifies internal and external threats
to the ethical integrity of HMN.

Threat modeling includes technical, social, and structural vectors.

---

## 1. External Threats

### A. Political Capture
Attempt to align HMN with ideological agenda.

Impact:
- Ethical distortion
- Value manipulation

Defense:
- Non-alignment policy
- Funding independence

---

### B. Economic Pressure
Funding dependency altering governance.

Impact:
- Silent compromise
- Prioritized scale over reflection

Defense:
- Diversified funding
- Transparency requirements

---

### C. Technical Exploitation
Exploiting trust or governance mechanisms.

Impact:
- Manipulated trust graphs
- Synthetic consensus

Defense:
- Anti-manipulation filters
- Conflict detection audits

---

## 2. Internal Threats

### A. Founder Drift
Original intent shifts subtly over time.

Impact:
- Ethical reinterpretation
- Power concentration

Defense:
- Charter binding
- Collective governance rights

---

### B. Moral Fatigue
Humans stop engaging in reflection.

Impact:
- Rubber-stamp decisions
- Delegation of responsibility to system

Defense:
- Reflection rituals
- Mandatory review cycles

---

### C. Normalization of Override
Frequent system overrides become routine.

Impact:
- Hidden authority
- Reduced accountability

Defense:
- Override reporting
- Independent review

---

## 3. Existential Threat

HMN itself becoming:
- A soft control infrastructure
- A moral authority substitute
- A system too complex to question

If this occurs,
dissolution must be considered.

---

Threat models must evolve.
But threats must never be minimized for comfort.
